Antigua, Guatemala

La Semana Santa

'Alfombras de aserrín', or sawdust carpets, are ornate pieces of art that also include palm fronds and flower petals. Families and artists create these spectacular bursts of color as part of Easter week festivities. The tradition originated with invading Spanish conquistadors in the 16th century, but Guatemalans have taken the artistry to a new level.
